👩 Mangalagaur & Kojagiri

Two of Maharashtra's most cherished women's festivals — celebrated by MMBA with song, dance, games, moonlit gatherings, and the warmth of feminine community spirit.

About These Festivals

Celebrating Maharashtrian Women

Mangalagaur is a Maharashtrian women's festival celebrated on every Tuesday (Mangalavar) during the holy month of Shravan. Newly married women pray to Goddess Gauri for the wellbeing of their husbands and family, accompanied by traditional songs (Mangalagaur geete), games (fugdya, tipree), and the lighting of 16 wicks. The atmosphere is joyful, playful, and distinctly Maharashtrian.

Kojagiri Poornima is the full moon night of Ashwin month — one of the most beautiful nights of the Maharashtrian calendar. Families stay up, sing songs to the moon (Chandrakoragiri), share spiced warm milk (masala dudh) and lotus seeds (makhana), and celebrate the harvest moon's beauty. The name "Kojagiri" comes from the goddess asking "Ko Jagari?" — who is awake tonight?

Mangalagaur Through the Years

2025
📅 August 2025 — Shravan Tuesdays
📍 Milpitas Community Space
Upcoming

MMBA Mangalagaur 2025 will be celebrated during Shravan (August 2025). Women of the MMBA community gather for traditional Mangalagaur songs, fugdya (traditional women's game), and puja. The evening is filled with laughter, music, and the warm energy of sisterhood.

  • 16-wick Gauri puja and aarti
  • Mangalagaur traditional songs — Geete and Ovya
  • Fugdya, Tipree, and Phugadya (traditional games)
  • Traditional dress — green bangles, nauvari saree
  • Homemade panchakhadya prasad
  • All women of the community welcome — no age limit

Past Edition

Mangalagaur 2024 was a memorable evening of song and sisterhood. The event saw participation from women across three generations — grandmothers teaching traditional songs to their grandchildren, creating a beautiful chain of cultural transmission. The fugdya session was particularly energetic and joyful.

  • Three-generation participation — toddlers to grandmothers
  • Live performance of Mangalagaur geete by senior members
  • Fugdya competition with prizes
  • Traditional Maharashtrian saree draping demonstration
  • Panchakhadya, ukadiche modak, and faral

Kojagiri Poornima — All Editions

Kojagiri Through the Years

2025
📅 October 6, 2025 (Ashwin Poornima)
📍 Outdoor Venue — Bay Area
Upcoming

Kojagiri Poornima 2025 falls on October 6. MMBA will host an evening celebration under the harvest moon with masala dudh (spiced warm milk), makhana (lotus seeds), traditional Kojagiri songs, and a serene moonlit gathering. Families are invited to come in traditional Maharashtrian attire.

  • Moonlit outdoor gathering at dusk
  • Masala dudh (spiced milk with saffron, cardamom, almonds)
  • Makhana (fox nuts) and dry fruit distribution
  • Kojagiri and Chandrakoragiri songs
  • Story of Kojagiri — cultural explanation for children
  • Bring a mat/blanket — moon-gazing encouraged!

Past Edition

Kojagiri 2024 was a magical evening — a clear night sky, a brilliant full moon, and the MMBA community gathered together sharing warm spiced milk, lotus seeds, and traditional songs. Children heard the legend of Kojagiri Poornima for the first time from community elders, carrying forward an ancient oral tradition.

  • Clear sky — full moon visible throughout the evening
  • Community elders narrated the Kojagiri legend
  • Three varieties of masala dudh — traditional, low-sugar, vegan
  • Makhana, dry fruits, and shrikhand prasad
  • Kojagiri songs sung by community choir

🪁 Makar Sankrant

Tilgul ghya, god god bola — MMBA's Makar Sankrant celebration brings kite flying, sesame sweets, and the warm Maharashtrian spirit of togetherness to Silicon Valley every January.

About Makar Sankrant

तिळगूळ घ्या, गोड गोड बोला

Makar Sankrant is one of the few Hindu festivals observed on a fixed solar date — January 14 — marking the sun's transition into Capricorn (Makara) and the end of winter. It's the harvest festival of Maharashtra, celebrated with patang (kite) flying, tilgul (sesame-jaggery sweets) exchange, and the traditional greeting "Tilgul ghya, god god bola" — Take this tilgul and speak sweetly. MMBA celebrates with a community gathering that includes live kite flying (Bay Area weather permitting), tilgul distribution, traditional Maharashtrian halwa, children's games, and cultural programs. Bajanwali ceremony and Bornahan for newly born babies in the community are also a cherished part of the celebration.

Makar Sankrant 2025 was a wonderful gathering of families on a clear January afternoon. Children flew kites from a large open field nearby while elders exchanged tilgul and blessings. The event featured a special Bornahan ceremony for 8 babies born to MMBA members in 2024, a beloved tradition that connects new families to the community.

  • Bornahan ceremony for 8 community babies — 2024 born

  • Children's traditional games competition
  • Sugarfree tilgul option for health-conscious members

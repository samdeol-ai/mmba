About Marathi Theater

A 100-Year Living Tradition

Maharashtra's modern theater (Natak) tradition dates back over a century, with roots in the Sangeet Natak (musical plays) of the 19th century. Marathi theater has produced legends like Bal Gandharva, Dinanath Mangeshkar, and more recently, actors like Nana Patekar, Dilip Prabhavalkar, and Subodh Bhave. MMBA honors this rich legacy by presenting an annual Marathi play performed by talented Bay Area community artists. Occasionally, visiting professional theatrical groups from India also perform at MMBA events. The natak is one of MMBA's most beloved annual traditions — a full evening of storytelling, emotion, and laughter in Marathi.

Special Productions

Visiting Artists from India

In addition to local productions, MMBA occasionally hosts professional theatrical groups visiting from Maharashtra. These performances bring acclaimed Marathi theater directly to Bay Area audiences — a rare treat for Maharashtrians living in the US.

Past visiting shows have featured celebrated Marathi plays and stand-up comedy. Follow @mmbayarea or contact us to be notified of upcoming visiting artist shows — these sell out quickly.

Festival of Lights

✨ Diwali Celebration

MMBA lights up the Bay Area every Diwali with rangoli competitions, cultural performances, traditional sweets, lamp-lighting ceremonies, and the warmth of community togetherness.

About This Festival

दीपावली — The Row of Lights

Diwali is the biggest festival of the Hindu calendar, celebrating the victory of light over darkness, knowledge over ignorance, and good over evil. MMBA's annual Diwali celebration is a multi-faceted community event featuring rangoli competitions, traditional aarti, Laxmi Pujan, Maharashtrian folk songs, cultural stage programs, firulki (firecrackers in safe settings), and the distribution of traditional Diwali faral (sweets and savories). Families gather in traditional attire — sarees, dhotis, and kurtas — creating a spectacular tableau of Maharashtrian culture in Silicon Valley.

2024
📅 November 1, 2024 (Diwali: Nov 1, 2024)
📍 Community Hall, Bay Area
Past Edition

Diwali 2024 was a glittering celebration of light, color, and community. The evening began with Laxmi Pujan, followed by a spectacular rangoli competition where participants created intricate patterns — the winning rangoli depicted the Bay Bridge lit in Diwali colors. The cultural program featured classical and folk dance performances, Marathi folk songs, and a comedy skit that had the audience in splits.

  • Rangoli competition with 30+ entries
  • Laxmi Pujan conducted by community pujari
  • Classical Bharatanatyam & Lavani performances
  • Marathi Diwali songs — Sughran Aali Angani
  • Diwali faral distribution — homemade by community members
  • Kandil exhibition — children's lantern display

2023
📅 November 11, 2023
📍 Bay Area — Milpitas area
Past Edition

Diwali 2023 was celebrated with vibrance and enthusiasm. The highlight was a special performance of traditional Diwali Pahat (Diwali at Dawn) songs by a local choir, recreating the unique Maharashtrian tradition of singing devotional songs before sunrise on Naraka Chaturdashi. The evening also featured a community dinner with traditional Diwali delicacies.

  • Diwali Pahat songs by MMBA community choir
  • Rangoli & kandil competition
  • Cultural stage program with folk dances
  • Diwali faral potluck — members brought homemade sweets
  • Community Laxmi Pujan

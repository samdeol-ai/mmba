🚶 Wari Procession

In devotion of Sant Dnyaneshwar and Sant Tukaram — MMBA's Wari brings the sacred Varkari pilgrimage tradition from Maharashtra to the streets of Silicon Valley.

The Varkari Tradition

माऊली माऊली — जय जय विठ्ठल

Wari (also known as Vari) is one of Maharashtra's most beloved spiritual traditions — a pilgrimage to Pandharpur in devotion of Lord Vitthal, undertaken by millions of Varkaris every year on Ashadhi and Kartiki Ekadashi. The procession is led by the palanquins of Sant Dnyaneshwar (from Alandi) and Sant Tukaram (from Dehu), followed by hundreds of thousands of devotees singing abhangas, carrying flags (dindi), and walking hundreds of kilometers. MMBA organizes a symbolic Wari procession in the Bay Area, allowing the community to participate in this extraordinary tradition — walking together, singing Vitthal's name, and experiencing the meditative peace of Varkari devotion.

Upcoming

MMBA Wari 2025 will coincide with Ashadhi Ekadashi (around early July 2025). The community walk will follow a designated Bay Area route, accompanied by abhanga singing, dindi procession, and the spirit of collective devotion. All are welcome — Varkari tradition is open to all devotees of Lord Vitthal.

  • Community walk on Ashadhi Ekadashi
  • Abhanga singing — Dnyaneshwar Mauli, Tukaram Maharaj
  • Dindi procession with traditional flags
  • Vitthal aarti at end of procession
  • Prasad — fruits and refreshments after walk
  • Comfortable walking shoes recommended

2024
📅 July 2024 — Ashadhi Ekadashi
📍 Bay Area
Past Edition

Wari 2024 drew over 100 participants for the community walk — the largest turnout in MMBA's Wari history. The procession was accompanied by a live harmonium and tabla, making the abhanga singing especially resonant. The walk concluded with a group aarti of Lord Vitthal and the distribution of prasad.

  • 100+ participants — record turnout
  • Live music accompaniment — harmonium & tabla
  • Abhangas by Dnyaneshwar, Tukaram, and Eknath Maharaj
  • Dindi procession with MMBA flags
  • Group Vitthal aarti at conclusion
  • Participants in traditional Varkari white attire
